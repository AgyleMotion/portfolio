Stretching of a Human Red Blood Cell in Abaqus/Explicit Using Hyperelastic Models Implemented in a VUMAT Subroutine

A user-defined material subroutine (VUMAT) was developed to implement the Skalak hyperelastic constitutive model for large-deformation membrane mechanics in Abaqus/Explicit. The subroutine computes stresses based on the stretch tensor and invariants of the right Cauchyâ€“Green deformation tensor, enabling accurate modeling of nonlinear elastic behavior in thin biological membranes such as human red blood cells. The VUMAT reads standard Abaqus/Explicit input variables, including deformation gradients, stretch tensors, material properties, and state variables, and updates stresses at each material point.

Abaqus/Explicit VUMAT Hyperelasticity Skalak Model

  • Conducted a mesh refinement study using four-node bilinear shell elements (S4R)
